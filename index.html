  Projects

Adsorption of Textile Industry Dye using Natural Adsorbent

This lab-scale project is aimed at assessing the efficacy of natural adsorbents in the treatment of wastewater from the textile industry. In the textile industry, a significant amount of dye ends up being lost during the dyeing process, leading to pollution. Since dyes are designed to be highly water soluble, they are difficult to remove using conventional wastewater treatment methods. Adsorption techniques can thus be used to treat these effluent streams. In this project, we used methyl red as an adsorbate and banana peel powder as an adsorbent to achieve the desired results. The results were generated based on two parameters, i.e., the effect of time duration on keeping adsorbent dose constant and the effect of adsorbent dose on keeping time duration constant.


Comparative Study Between Rankine Cycle and Brayton Power Plant Cycle

The purpose of this project was to compare two thermodynamic cycles used in power plants, the Rankine cycle and the Brayton cycle. The working fluid for the Rankine cycle was steam and the working fluid for the Brayton cycle was supercritical carbon dioxide. We compared the construction and operation of the cycle and discussed various processes and equations. Additionally, we discussed a suitable substitute for the working fluid in both cycles. The actual Rankine cycle and Brayton cycle were also compared along with energy balances, boiler energy loss, capital cost, and technical challenges. Results for the project were found along with the current research on both cycles.
